Current Project: The Darkness Within

A pixel art horror game inspired primarily by Alone in the Dark

Projected Release Date: Late 2025

August 4th, 2024

Progress on The Darkness Within is coming along well! Still in the prototyping and basic mechanics phase, but certain mechanics are taking me a lot less time to implement than I had initially thought they would, which I'm choosing to interpet as a good sign. Honestly, it's surprising just how much of the work I did on Frosveld is transferring almost 1:1 to this project; the work I did on it then has really been a massive time-saver so far. In any case, hopefully two weeks from now I'll have some half-way decent pictures and gifs to post on here.

Speaking of half-way decent visuals, I don't know when I'll be able to afford an artist to make the game actually aesthetically pleasing or a sound designer to design the sounds, but my current plan has me reaching out to publishers towards the end of this year/start of next year, so hopefully by then, if all goes well.

Lastly, I was recently perusing the rules for Iron & Aether, as I am wont to do on occassion, and noticed several errata in the Aetherian class that demanded fixing, so there's a new patch out today that mostly focuses on that. I hope anyone playing the game enjoys!

That's pretty much it for this update guys. Thanks again, see you in a couple weeks!
Griffin G. Salazar

July 22nd, 2024

After a great amount of reflection, I've decided that developing Cemetary Sentinels would not be a worth-while effort. This is because I strongly believe that it would not be successful, and is extremely unlikely to even get published in the first place. Additionally, and just as if not more importantly, I am utterly sick of working on games that have no story and have nothing to say. Iron Guard, Frosveld, Cemetary Sentinels - I want to be a game developer because I want to tell stories, so why haven't I been doing that?.

Well, it's time for that to change. The Darkness Within is going to be different from all of my previous projects: it will have passion and inspiration behind it, and will be the first of the stories I tell through video games. My current goal now is to get a polished demo up and running - roughly the first third of Chapter 1 - and then use that to find a publisher or crowdfund development. With any luck, that will happen by the end of this year, and then the game will hopefully be done in Q4 of 2025 - hell, a Halloween release would be perfect, but I'm not planning on that. This is easily the biggest project I've ever undertaken, and I wouldn't be surprised if hoping for a 2025 release turns out to be unrealistic.

In any case, I'm really excited to be working on this, to finally be making a game with real substance, to finally be telling a story! I hope anyone reading this is just as excited as I am. While the game is being developed, I'll be sharing my progress on here, and keeping y'all updated on how things are going behind-the-scenes, such as when I find an artist or if circumstances prevent me from working for however long.

Thank you, and I'll see you in August!
Griffin G. Salazar

Current Project: Cemetary Sentinels

A Plants vs. Zombies-inspired 2D autobattler about spirits attempting to break out of a cemetary

Projected Release Date: October 28th, 2024

July 7th, 2024

As you might be able to tell, development of Frosveld has been cancelled. The primary reason for this is that, as it turns out, I am one degree of separation from the head of the indie horror publishing studio DreadX! In light of this news, I have decided to completely refocus my efforts for the month of July into putting together a prototype/demo for a Halloween-themed game in the hopes of securing funding for it. This project - Cemetary Sentinels , as you might have guessed - is one I've had circulating in the back of my brain for a while now, and I had originally intended on releasing it for Halloween of 2025; however, I will gladly take this opportunity to move my plans forward a full year! In the event this fails, I have another horror project in mind that is much less time-sensitive, and that I'm positive I could secure funding for should, again, a working prototype be produced. To be perfectly honest, I feel rather foolish for not pursuing getting funding from a publisher much sooner. For so long I'd been adverse to the idea of working with one, this whole time believing that going fully grassroots with self-funding and Kickstarter to be the only - or at least, the best - way to go. And while that can definitely still happen in the future, with the benefit of this new perspective, I think pursuing the aid of an established publisher is definitely the better option, at least for now.

However, before work on this project can begin in earnest, I've come to a stark realization: Iron Guard needs to be fixed. It is my only released game of any note (not counting Iron & Aether of course), and it is a buggy mess. I am firmly of the belief that any publisher who takes a look at it would be a fool to place any stock in me or my capabilities, and I would be hard-pressed to quell their doubts. So, while I do believe there are flaws inherent in the design of Iron Guard that will prevent it from ever being a stand-out game, that doesn't mean I can't take a few days to fix the more superficial flaws, and make myself appear that much better by association. Ergo, that will be the first order of business, and then work on Cemetary Sentinels can truly get started.

Lastly, I've decided it would be prudent to update this blog on the 1st and 3rd Sundays of each month, instead of just the 1st.

Thank you, and I'll see you in two weeks!
Griffin G. Salazar

Current Project: Frosveld

A retro-inspired 2D dungeon crawler with mild roguelike elements

June 2nd, 2024

From here on out, I intend to update this blog on the first Sunday of every month in perpetuity, giving details on the progress of what I've worked on for the past month, and what I intend to work on in the coming month.
To start, in November of last year, I started working on a project called Frosveld, an open-world dungeon crawler heavily inspired by the oldest of the old-school RPGs, in the vain of DnD, Alkalabeth, Wizardry, and others. I needed something I could work on that would cost me little-to-no money while I got my finances in order, and I decided to challenge myself to release 2 games in 2024, of which I wanted Frosveld to be one. Unfortunately, it seems I may have overscoped a bit, and may have to put off releasing Frosveld until early 2025, seeing as I want to release a game for the Christmas season, so that will be the focus of the later months of the year. I believe to retain a 2024 release date for Frosveld, I may very well have to grind myself into dust.

That being said, I do intend to continue working on it until the planned release date, most of the reason being that working on it has been an exceptionally educational experience for me. Ugly as the game might be, I have learnt so much about developing game systems I had never previously touched on before, and grown my own capabilities as a programmer and game developer far beyond what they were previously. Even if I were to not make a single red cent off of Frosveld, my time spent on it will not have been in vain when taking into account my own personal growth and all that I have learned.

So, by the time the next blog update comes around, I intend to have all abilities, leveling up, dungeon layouts, and world persistence all fully functioning, as well as a logo for the game and pictures and gifs of all of this.

Thank you, and I'll see you in a month!
