Chapter 1: Aether

What is Aether?

Aether is a truly wondrous substance – or rather, a collection of substances with powerful and unique properties. It is the physical manifestation of the six elements, and though not used for much, what it is used for is the foundation of the world, history, and workings of the world of Aethra (occasionally used as an example herein) and the I&A ruleset.

Types of Aether – The Six Elements

There are six types of Aether in existence, one for each element, and they are all briefly described here. Each element is further divided into two sub-elements, and when these sub-elements intermingle, pseudo-elements are formed; both of these are described in full later on.

Dark. The element of emptiness, silence, the void, and all the secrets that lie within; its sub-elements are Void and Frost. Darkness is Light’s complement, and without it all would be just as blind as though there were darkness without light. Dark Aether is a deep black with occasional hints of blue.

Light. The element of knowledge, of hope, of the sun shining through the trees on a beautiful day; its sub-elements are Radiance and Fire. Light cannot exist without its counterpart, darkness, as they are co-dependent on each other to maintain harmony in the universe. Light Aether is a bright white with hints of yellow and red.

Magic. The element of illusions and power, of wonder, lies, and all which is not as it seems and does not act as it should; its sub-elements are Illusion and Force. Magic is believed to be the source of all creativity, and who is to say those who believe such are wrong? Magic Aether is purple and glimmers frequently.

Soul. The element responsible for everything that lives and dies, it is Soul that creates that spark that generates life in the universe - and it is Soul that necessitates that all life must come to an end; its sub-elements are Life and Death. Soul Aether varies from an ethereal green to a sickly grey.

Space. The element of all which physically exists and has matter; its sub-elements are Earth and Air. Space Aether is clear and translucent, and appears to shift and obscure the area immediately around and behind it.

Time. Flowing ever-forward like a river, Time ensures everything in the universe keeps moving, progressing, improving, deteriorating; whatever its natural course may be, Time ensures everything will meet its destiny when it is meant to, and its sub-elements are Possibility and Reality. Time Aether is also clear and translucent, however everything around it seems to move a bit more quickly or slowly than it should.

The Sub-Elements

Each of the six elements has two distinct aspects - the two sides of the proverbial elemental coin. These sub-elements represent the two halves of the whole element, creating a Yin and Yang of primordial power that, when combined, form the fundamental force of nature that is the element itself. When one sub-element "interacts" with an adjacent sub-element, that interaction creates a pseudo-element; these are explained in greater detail in the next section. Given here is a brief explanation of the sub-elements themselves, arranged by adjancency rather than alphabetically.

Light-Fire. The aspect of Light that provides heat, its presence can be both comforting and horribly painful; it is adjacent to Magic-Force, creating the Lightning pseudo-element.

Light-Radiance. The part of Light that is pure, well, light, illuminating but also blinding; it is adjacent to Dark-Void, creating the Twilight pseudo-element.

Dark-Void. The pure, unyielding blackness of Dark, blinding, but also protecting and comforting in its privacy; it is adjacent to Light-Radiance, forming Twilight.

Dark-Frost. The cold aspect of Dark, chilling to the bone and cruelly biting those caught unprepared; it is adjacent to Soul-Death, forming the pseudo-element Decay.

Soul-Death. The part of Soul that brings about the end of all living things, ensuring that, for better or worse, nothing lasts forever; it is adjacent to Dark-Frost, manifesting into Decay.

Soul-Life. The aspect of Soul that animates and bestows willpower, autonomy, self-awareness into an otherwise dull and stagnant universe; it is adjacent to Space-Earth, creating Water.

Space-Earth. A bit of a misnomer, Space-Earth does not just refer to actual dirt and soil, but all the solid physical material of the universe; it is adjacent to Soul-Life, making up one-half of the Water pseudo-element.

Space-Air. The part of Space that refers to all that which is not occupied by physical material; it is adjacent to Time-Reality, forming the ephemeral pseudo-element of Memory.

Time-Reality. That which is happening and has happened, forever part of this universe's history; it is adjacent to Space-Air, manifesting in the pseudo-element of Memory.

Time-Possibility. That which has not happened but may, or which didn't happen but could have, if things had been just a bit different; it is adjacent to Magic-Illusion, forming the pseudo-element of Prophecy.

Magic-Illusion. The duplicitous aspect of the ethereal Magic, displaying pure imagination and making fools and wonderers of those in its presence; it is adjacent to Time-Possibility, creating Prophecy.

Magic-Force. The solid and undeniable side of the powerful Magic, bending those who feel it to its will and forcing them to revel in its glory; it is adjacent to Light-Fire, forming Lightning.

The Pseudo-Elements

All of the elements are, in a sense, related. They are the fundamental forces of the universe, forming its very core, its systems, its beating heart. They are like the siblings of one big cosmic family - and much like actual siblings, some are closer with each other than others; in a sense, they are "adjacent". Picture it like this: if the elements were to form a wheel or ring, they would be arranged thusly: Light-Dark-Soul-Space-Time-Magic-Light. Where the sub-elements "meet" are the pseudo-elements, natural forces that bridge the gaps between the elements, and in so doing are an intrinsic part of both of them, hence why Aetherians are able to cast spells derived from the pseudo-elements. Each of the pseudo-elements are explained in brief here.

Lightning (Light-Fire + Magic-Force). When the pure scorching heat of Fire and the unyielding power of Force combine, Lightning is created, searing all in its path with its might.

Twilight (Light-Radiance + Dark-Void). When the day meets the night, and Light and Dark mingle in their supposed opposition, the glowing darkness and enshrouding light of Twilight comes to be.

Decay (Dark-Frost + Soul-Death). As things approach their end, they grow cold and begin to whither away, slowly succumbing to the forces of Decay.

Water (Soul-Life + Space-Earth). Life given form and structure, Water occurs when the ethereal nature of Soul and the physicality of Space combine.

Memory (Space-Air + Time-Reality). As that which was and has come to pass fades off into nothing, solidifying its place in the timeline of the universe, it becomes as ephemeral as the Air Sentients breathe, becoming Memory.

Prophecy (Time-Possibility + Magic-Illusion). When events that may yet be project themselves onto the imaginative and gossamer nature of Illusion, Prophecy is born.

Elemental Storms

Elemental Storms are, as the name would imply, storms that unleash in their fury the powers of one of the elements. What exactly triggers an Elemental Storm is up to the GM, but as an example, on Aethra the most common cause of Elemental Storms is when the barriers between the planes become worn thin in a given area, though what exactly causes that is still a mystery to Sentient minds. And who knows what may happen if one of those barriers were to break or wear away entirely...

The nature of these storms varies according to the nature of the element: Light storms, for example, may involve blooms of sourceless light filling an area, fire falling from the sky like rain, lightning erupting out of nowhere; on the other hand, a Time storm may involve a group of people having visions of the past and/or future, suddenly growing older or younger, or skipping backwards or forwards in time. While bombardments of the element itself are to be naturally expected, because all of the elements are, to some degree, omnipresent, the appropriate pseudo-elements will also make appearances during Elemental Storms, e.g. lightning will strike during both Light and Magic storms, heavy torrents of rain will fall during both Soul and Space storms, etc. In any case, Elemental Storms are usually quite dangerous and terrifying events, ranging from fractured buildings, minds, and/or bodies to cataclysmic losses of life and property in the affected area. Typically, these storms last from 1-6 days and cover up to 20 square kilometers.

While Elemental Storms are incredibly dangerous, they are not without their benefits: the bursts and manifestations of elemental power typically leave behind rich deposits of Aether, and children born during an Aether Storm have decent chances (about 20% each) of becoming Aetherians or Elementia of the corresponding element.

Forms of Aether

All types of Aether can take three forms: solid, which is typically crystalline when pure or ore-like when impure; liquid, which is thin and water-like when pure or thick and sludgy when impure; and gaseous, which is always thick and semi-opaque. Pure solid Aether is surprisingly edible, though most who wish to ingest it do prefer to drink it when possible. One pound of solid Aether is roughly a few centimeters in diameter.

To convert solid Aether into liquid, it must be crushed and ground into what would normally be a fine and sprinkle-like dust, but in the case of Aether liquifies and creates a liquid with the same volume as its solid counterpart. To convert liquid into gaseous, it must be boiled at a temperature of 100 degrees Alchelite, the same that it takes to boil water; however, gaseous Aether is not hot and can be interacted with safely, though breathing it in for a few minutes is enough to induce Aether Sickness. Gaseous Aether cannot be converted into liquid, but it can be made solid by exposing it to a temperature of 0°A (also the freezing temperature of water).

Alchelite (A). Alchelite is the temperature-measuring metric in I&A. For simplicity, it is equal to the real-world metric of Celsius. Sentient body temperature is 37°A.

Aether Sickness

When one consumes or ingests any kind of pure Aether or injects it into their bloodstream, they become afflicted with Aether Sickness. Every day for the next 1d3+1 days, Aether Sickness reduces the afflicted creature’s Health by 20% of their maximum, during which time the afflicted is sickened and cannot regain any Health by any means. If this disease brings the afflicted’s Health down to 0, they must succeed on a DR 15 Fortitude Save or die as the Aether consumes them and transforms them into a monster of the corresponding Aether type, e.g. Magic Aether will create a Fae or Vi, Dark Aether will create a Hollow or Frozen, and so on.

Should an Aetherian ingest Aether in any way, if the Aether is not of the type that they have already bonded to, they will invariably be riddled with Aether Sickness for the next day or two, during which time they will intermittently vomit up the Aether they consumed in solid and/or liquid form. If it is of the type they have bonded with, then their Mana is restored by an amount equal to 5 times the pounds of Aether that they consumed.

Where to Find It

Aether is found in a variety of places, but on the Central Plane it is most commonly found in four places:
1. In subterranean deposits rife with veins and "natural" clusters;
2. In large chunks and pillars found seemingly randomly throughout the world, such as quarries and great boulders;
3. In isolated lakes, ponds, natural fountains, and the like, again scattered seemingly at random throughout the world;
4. In the aftermath of Elemental Storms.

Though most of these sources would at first appear finite in nature, it is not unknown for sources of Aether to - either spontaneously or over time - replenish themselves, or for new sources to appear where there previously were none. Additionally, each element's plane is absolutely plentiful with the corresponding type of Aether.

Where to Buy It

In the world of Aethra, the acquisition and distribution of Aether is typically handled by whoever discovered it, or whoever is in control of a given source at any given time. This may be the local government, a rich merchant, or some farmer who discovered Aether growing in their crops. Each of these distributors may charge different prices for the Aether in their possession, and at that point it is up to the GM to adjudicate exact prices. If a type of Aether is abundant in a given area, a good baseline for its price is 25A/pound, and this price would of course only increase as it became more rare and/or more desired by the buyer.

What it is Used For

Aether is a mysterious and powerful substance, and though its uses are actually quite limited, what it is used for is fundamental for Sentient societies, civilizations, and cultures.

Aether’s most basic and fundamental use is the creation of Aetherians. Aside from that, Aether is primarily used in alchemy, though it has a tertiary use in the creation and decoration of jewelry - i.e., instead of using diamonds, rubies, or the like for shows of wealth and status, Sentients instead use Aether.

Dark. Dark Aether is used to brew concoctions and enchant items that create darkness, cause silence, curse with blindness, or inflict some sort of freezing effect.

Light. Light’s most common use is for the creation of light-emitting enchants, particularly effective truth serums, and anything and everything to do with fire (Light and Magic together are required for lightning-based creations).

Magic. Magic Aether is very useful in the creation of deceitful, double-natured enchants, hallucinogenic poisons, and enchants that manipulate the forces of the world.

Soul. Soul is an incredibly versatile source of power, as it is used to both create Health potions to restore life and various poisons to end it.

Space. Space Aether is incredibly versatile in the world of alchemy, being used in enchants that both bestow strength, fortitude, and durability, and in those that grant the ability to fly, enhance one's reflexes, or allow one to breathe safely when they otherwise would not be.

Time. Used in the creation of hasting and slowing concoctions, time-manipulating enchanted items, and items and tinctures that bestow visions of the past or future. Also used to create exceptionally accurate clocks.

Aether Effects on Non-Sentients

When ingested by a non-Sentient creature such as a Beast, the Aether will corrupt their body and turn them into a creature of the corresponding Aether type, e.g. consuming Light Aether will create a Radiant or Scorched, Soul Aether will create an Undead or Unliving, etc.

Anti-Aether - Antitherium

Antitherium is a special metal that nullifies the arcane powers and effects of Aether. Generally, when Antitherium is used to forge something or integrated into something's construction, it will nullify any arcane effects that occur inside of or adjacent to the object. For example, a building built with Antitherium will negate any and all spells cast inside it, nor can it be teleported into or out of. Manacles, shackles, or collars made of Antitherium may prevent Aetherians (or other spellcasting creatures) from casting spells entirely, depending on the placement and number of the restraints and the concentration of Antitherium in them.

Armor made of Antitherium will grant the wearer advantage on saves against spell effects, as well as grant casters disadvantage on damage rolls against the wearer. Weapons made of Antitherium are a truly interesting case - for one, casters have disadvantage on saves to maintain Concentration when struck with such a weapon; for two, if an Aetherian is reduced to 0 Health or otherwise qualifies for a Lingering Injury when hit with an Antitherium weapon, they may suffer an effect that debilitates their ability to cast spells for some length of time (e.g. 1d6 days). Some examples are: not being able to cast spells at all, not being able to maintain Concentration, having their maximum Mana temporarily reduced, or possibly even losing an Aetherian level.

For mechanical intents and purposes (e.g. blacksmithing), consider Antitherium equal to steel.
