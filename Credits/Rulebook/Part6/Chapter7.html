Chapter 7: Weather

Weather Effects

Weather has an especially interesting influence on the life of those affected by it, especially for travelers, which adventurers frequently are. Given here is a list of various weathers, followed by a brief description that includes how it affects the immediate environment. Aether Storms are covered here.


In clear weather terrain is normal and everything is relatively easy to see.


Extreme cold bites through straight to the bone, freezing a creature’s blood in their veins and granting them a cruel and macabre death. While in extreme cold, a creature takes final freeze damage every hour depending on their state of dress: if they are wearing no or light clothes, they take an amount of freeze damage equal to 40% of their maximum Health; if they are wearing Light armor, they take 30% of their; max as freeze damage; with Medium armor, they take 20%; and in Heavy armor and heavy clothing, they take 10%. If wet flesh makes contact with extremely cold metal, the flesh becomes stuck to the metal until either a. the metal is warmed up enough for the flesh to become unstuck or b. the flesh is ripped off, dealing anywhere between 1 and 1d6 true damage depending on how much flesh was stuck.


Should a creature be traveling through an area of intense and extreme heat, they will take final burn damage every hour depending on what they are wearing: if they are wearing nothing or light clothes, they take an amount of burn damage equal to 10% of their maximum Health; if they are wearing Light armor, they take 20% of their max as burn damage; for Medium armor and heavy clothing, they take 30%; and for Heavy armor, they take 40%. If a creature is taking steps to help cool themselves off, such as wearing reflective clothing or constantly drinking water, they take the next lowest tier of.


During lightning storms, any hearing-based Perception checks are made with disadvantage, and visibility is reduced to just a couple miles. Should a creature be struck by lightning, they take 4d6 final electric damage.


Rain causes everything in the immediate environment to be slick, creating difficult terrain – whether it be slippery cobblestones, sloppy and thick mud, or slick and slushy snow and ice. It also lowers visibility to just a mile and douses all unprotected and non-arcane Small or smaller flames. Depending on the severity of the rain, Perception checks may be made normally, with disadvantage, or may automatically fail as appropriate.


Similar to rain, snow creates difficult terrain in the affected area as everything becomes covered in snow and ice. Depending on the severity of the snow, visibility may be reduced to a few kilometers/couple miles or just a few meters/feet, and Perception checks are made normally, with disadvantage, or automatically fail as appropriate. All unprotected and non-arcane Small or smaller flames are doused.


Storms of all kinds severely impede movement, causing all movement to take twice as long as it otherwise would, and reducing visibility to a kilometer/half a mile at best and just a few meters/feet at worst. Medium or smaller unprotected and non-arcane flames are doused, and all ranged attacks and Perception checks on targets < 9m/30ft away are made with disadvantage; ranged attacks and Perception checks on targets > 9m/30ft away automatically fail.


Wind causes all Tiny unprotected and non-arcane flames to be doused immediately and causes all ranged attacks to be made with disadvantage. Visibility and terrain are unaffected, but hearing-based Perception checks are made with disadvantage.
