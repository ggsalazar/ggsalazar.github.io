Chapter 1: Actions

Characters are able to perform a variety of actions as they explore and interact with the world during their adventures. These actions can be performed in or out of combat, and many can be performed as reactions.

During each round of combat, a creature can perform up to a number of actions on their turn determined by their DEX score and 1 reaction during any other creature’s turn. Certain class features, spells, enchanted items, or other special circumstances may grant a creature more actions or reactions.

Holding Actions

If a creature so desires, instead of using all of their actions on their turn, they can instead hold up to two of their actions while they wait for a specific trigger, which they must state at the time they decide to hold these actions; if this trigger occurs, they then use their reaction to perform their held actions; if it doesn't, they lose those actions, but can use their reaction for something else if they desire.


Action Cost: Varies

When a creature takes this action, they are choosing to attack a creature, character, or object within range using their currently equipped weaponry, their spells, or whatever else they may wish to use. When making an attack with a weapon, unless the weapon has the “DEX” property, add your STR score to the roll.

Typically, attacking once requires the use of a single action, however there are some weapons that require more than 1 action to be used effectively. If you do not have enough actions at your disposal to effectively attack with this weapon, you can use the rest of your remaining actions to attack with it once with disadvantage.


Action Cost: 2

To blind a creature, the blinder must make a DEX check contested against the target’s Reflex save. On a success, the target is blinded until they use 2 actions to remove the blinding element from their eyes.


Action Cost: 2

Whatever weapons a character has in their equipment slots at any given point are their equipped weapons, and they can attack with only those weapons unless they swap out one weapon for another.


Action Cost: 2

To disarm a creature, the disarmer must make a Sleight of Hand or STR check contested by the DEX or STR check of the creature they are attempting to disarm. On a success, the weapon is plucked out of their hand and thrown 1d3 meters away in a direction of the disarmer’s choosing. The weapon can be caught by you or another person in the weapon’s path with a successful DR 15 Sleight of Hand check, provided the being attempting to catch it has an empty hand and their reaction.


Action Cost: 2

A creature disengages when they wish to leave the melee range of all opponents they are currently in melee range of without provoking any opportunity attacks. If they enter a new opponent’s melee range, they must disengage again if they wish to continue avoiding opportunity attacks.


Action Cost: 2

Evading is a being’s way of preparing for an incoming attack or harmful effect and bracing themselves against it. When a creature evades, their Dodge score is doubled and all attacks and damage-dealing effects have disadvantage against them until the start of their next turn; they also have advantage on all Reflex saves for the same period.


Action Cost: 2

When choosing to grapple another creature, the grappler must make a STR or DEX check contested against the Reflex save of the target. On a success, the target is grappled by the grappler, and may use 2 actions on their turn to make a STR or DEX check to free themselves contested against the grappler’s STR or DEX check. Alternatively, you can choose to restrain the target, though you cannot take other actions while restraining them.


Action Cost: 2

A helping creature make the same check as the creature they are helping, and adds half of the result (rounded down to the nearest half) to the result of their check.


Action Cost: 2

The Hide action is used when a creature wishes to avoid being spotted by another entity. In order to hide, a creature needs something to hide behind/in and must make a Stealth check contested by the searching party’s Perception check.


Action Cost: 2

Your maximum jump height in meters is equivalent to 1/3 your STR score, while your maximum jump length is equal to half your STR score; with a running start, it becomes your full STR score. A running start requires a character to run uninterrupted for at least 4m in a straight line before the jump.


Action Cost: Varies

This is arguably the most loosely-definable action, since it can apply to almost infinitely different situations. This action is used when a creature wishes to interact with their environment in such a way that is not otherwise described here; examples include propping a chair up against a door, flipping a table over to use it as cover, or smashing through a glass pane. It is up to the GM whether the action a creature wishes to improvise is doable and, if it is, how difficult it is and how many actions it takes to do.


Action Cost: 1+

You move in a direction of your choosing, to a distance of your choosing, so long as you have enough movement to get there and nothing is in your way. Moving through difficult terrain and moving while prone requires twice as much movement as moving through normal terrain, unless otherwise stated by a special exception. For example, if a character with 10m of movement tried to move through difficult terrain, they could only move through 5m of it at most. The amount of movement at your disposal is determined by your Size and AGI score.

The amount of movement a creature has assumes they are dedicating their entire turn to moving; to determine how much you can move using an action, simply divide your movement by the number of actions you have. For example, a creature with 24 meters of movement and 4 actions could use just 1 action to move up to 6 meters, allowing them to use their remaining actions for something else.

Lastly, it is important to note that when you decide to use an action to move, the movement does not all need to be used at once and can be broken up; you could, for instance, use a single action to move 3 meters, use another to attack once, and then move another 3 meters without expending another action.


Action Cost: Varies

Using almost any weapon and/or appropriate item, a creature can pin another to a surface, usually by impaling their clothes to that surface or weighing them down with something heavy. When a creature takes this action, they must make a STR/DEX check contested against the target’s Fortitude/Reflex save (determined by what check the pinner is making): if the pinner wins the contest, the target is considered grappled by whatever is pinning them, and can take 2 actions on their next turn to make a STR/DEX check to free themselves. The DR for this check is the result of the pinner’s original STR/DEX check.

The action cost to do this varies depending on what is being used: if using a weapon, the action cost is the same as the weapon, and if using an item found in the immediate environment, the action cost is 2.


Action Cost: 2

To shove a creature, make a STR check contested against their attempted victim’s Fortitude/Reflex save; on a success, they are shoved a number of meters equal to twice the shover’s STR score (minimum 1) in a direction of their choice.


Action Cost: Varies

When one creature wishes to speak to another, whether by verbal communication or otherwise, they can do so without using any of their actions, unless the GM rules otherwise.


Action Cost: 2

A creature stands up when they are prone and no longer want to be.


Action Cost: 1

To tackle a creature, one must first have moved in a straight line towards their target for > 4m, at which point the target must make a Fort./Ref. save against the tackler's STR check or be knocked prone and become grappled by the tackler.


Action Cost: 1

To trip another creature, make a STR or DEX check contested against their Fortitude or Reflex save; on a success, they are knocked prone.


Action Cost: 1+

This action is performed whenever a creature wishes to use an item that can be found either in their immediate environment or their inventory. A couple of examples would be quaffing a potion (2 actions) or opening a container (1 action). If the item is large and/or would take a large amount of time and/or effort to use, then it may require more than one action to perform.

Reactions

Reactions are performed in immediate response to something in the environment; every creature has at least 1 reaction at their disposal each round, which can be used on any turn other than their own. As stated above, there are some features that add to a character’s list of possible reactions, but those are not described here.


An opportunity attack, or attack of opportunity, can be performed whenever a creature leaves the melee range of another creature, allowing them to take the chance to cause them injury, provided the weapon they are attacking with only takes 1 action to attack with. The Disengage action can be used to avoid attacks of opportunity.


Whenever a character holds up to 2 actions, they can then perform them whenever the trigger that they determine is activated.
