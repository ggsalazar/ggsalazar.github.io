Classless Feats

Classless feats, which can be taken in lieu of Ability Score Improvements, serve as a way to give your character unique abilities or enhance their existing ones in some way. They can make characters stand out, and their uses range from the martial to the social to the explorative. Given here is a list of feats that can be learned by any character of any class.



You learn 1 additional Novice feat from a class that you have learnt at least 1 Novice feat from. This feat can be taken multiple times.


You learn 1 additional Journeyman feat from a class that you have learnt or had the opportunity to learn at least 1 Journeyman feat from. This feat can be taken multiple times.


You learn 1 additional Expert feat from a class that you have learnt or had the opportunity to learn at least 1 Expert feat from. This feat can be taken multiple times.


You learn 1 additional Master feat from a class that you have learnt or had the opportunity to learn at least 1 Master feat from.


Prerequisite: >2 Alchemy Supplies proficiency
After thorough practice, experimentation, and training in the uses and workings of alchemy, you gain the following benefits:
  • You gain 1 proficiency with Alchemy Supplies, to a maximum of 5;
  • The amount of Ingredients you require to brew a concoction is reduced by 1 (minimum 1);
  • The number of days it takes you to prepare a concoction of any type is reduced by an amount equal to your Alchemy Supplies proficiency, to a minimum of 6 hours;
  • The number of days it takes you to prepare an enchant of any type is reduced by an amount equal to double your Alchemy Supplies proficiency, to a minimum of 1 day.

You keep a sharp eye out for anything that would do you harm, granting you the following benefits:
  • Your Perception proficiency increases by 1, to a maximum of 5;
  • You gain a +5 bonus to initiative;
  • For the purposes of combat, you cannot be surprised.

You specialize in slaying those with arcane powers, granting you the following benefits:
  • Whenever a spell-casting creature casts a spell within range of one of your equipped weapons, you can use your reaction to make a single attack against them;
  • When you deal final damage to a spell-casting creature that is concentrating on a spell, they have disadvantage on their save to maintain concentration;
  • You have advantage on saves against spells.

You’ve got a strong and capable body, granting you the following benefits:
  • You take 1d6 less damage from fatigue;
  • You gain 1 proficiency in both Climb and Swim, to a maximum of 5.

You gain a Beast companion that you have trained to fight by your side. This companion, which must be Medium or smaller and Tier 0 or 1, acts on its own turn in combat and gains the following features:
  • It is exceptionally loyal to you and will not betray you unless forced to do so via exceptional means, such as by a spell;
  • its Health is equal to its original Health or 5 times your total level, whichever is larger;
  • if it can hear you, you can use an action to verbally command it to perform up to 2 actions (or more if you're commanding it to move), which it performs to the best of its ability.
Should your companion die, you can spend three day’s rests training a new one to replace it.


If you use all of your actions on your turn to move, you can also make a single melee weapon attack; if you move at least 4m in a straight line prior to making this attack, you can add your proficiency with the weapon you are attacking with to the damage of the attack.


Prerequisite: > 1 level in Mutant or Rogue and < 2 levels in Warrior

In the Warrior class description, under the 3rd level feature “Combat Maneuvers”, you may select any 3 abilities from the given list. Between rests, you can use your chosen maneuvers a number of times equal to half your Mutant, Rogue, or Warrior level, rounded up, and regain 1 use at the end of a quick rest. You can use only one maneuver per attack or reaction.


You’ve got a lot of practice fighting with a weapon in each hand. From now on, while wielding a weapon in each hand, your Armor score increases by an amount equal to half your proficiency with those weapons added together (minimum 1).


Prerequisite: Medicine Kits proficiency >3

After seeing more than your fair share of combat and spending countless hours healing the wounded, you gain the following benefits:
  • You gain 1 proficiency with Medicine Kits, to a maximum of 5;
  • You gain the ability to use medicine kits at the cost of 4 actions instead of 10 minutes, though this only restores an amount of Health equal to half the result of your Medicine Kit check; taking the full 10 minutes to use it utilizes the full extent of your check.

Your soul is possessed of an incredibly strong and indomitable discipline, increasing your Willpower beyond the norm. From now on, do not half your CHA and chosen score when determining your Willpower score; if you already gain this benefit from another source, your Willpower save instead increases by another 2.


Prerequisite: >3 CHA

You’ve mastered the art of rallying your allies’ morale and raising their spirits. You can now spend 1 minute giving an inspiring speech to those around you, hardening their resolve to fight. At the end of your speech, pick up to 6 creatures other than yourself that heard you and are within 10m of you: you and each of those creatures gain an amount of temporary Health equal to your level + your CHA score, which lasts until it is expended or until their next night's or day's rest. This can be done once between night's or days's rests.


You have learned to become quite observant of your opponents, granting you the following benefits:
  • Your Perception proficiency increases by 1, up to 5;
  • You can take one minute to observe a creature and must make a Perception check thereafter. Divide that number by 10 and round appropriately (up if half), and that is the number of things that you learn about them from the following list: DEF score, any one Ability Score, any one save score, their level & class(es), any one of their Weapon Skill proficiencies, or any one of their skill proficiencies. You can do this to a single creature multiple times, requiring an additional minute of observation and an additional check each time.

You’re very cognizant of the world around you and your location in the world, granting you the following benefits:
  • You always know which way is North, granting you advantage on Navigation checks;
  • If you can see the sun or the moon, you know the current time to the nearest quarter hour;
  • You can accurately recall anything you have seen or heard within the past 15 days.

You’ve got a knack for languages and the way people speak and understand each other, granting you the following benefits:
  • You learn 3 Sentient languages, or 1 Elemental language and 1 Sentient language;
  • You are able to create written ciphers, and any creatures whom you designate are able to read and understand your encrypted writings. Any creature you don’t designate must be able to read the language you wrote in and must succeed on an INT check with a DR equal to 5 times your INT score to decipher your writings over the course of an hour if they succeed on the check, or a full day if they get a Partial Success.

You gain 3 luck points that you can use to grant yourself advantage on any attack, check, or save, or grant another creature disadvantage on an attack against you, a check against something you are doing or have done, or a save against an effect caused by you. You regain all expended luck points at the end of a day's rest and regain a single luck point at the end of a night's rest.


Prerequisite: Alchemist and Alchemy Supplies proficiency >5

Your skill in alchemy is the stuff of legend. Because of your discipline and dedication to your studies, you gain the following benefits:
  • Your Alchemy Supplies proficiency increases to 6 if it hasn't already;
  • The amount of Ingredients you require to brew concoctions is reduced by another 2 (minimum 1);
  • The number of days it takes you to prepare a concoction is further reduced by an amount equal to your Alchemy Supplies proficiency (minimum 3 hours);
  • The number of days it takes you to prepare an enchant is further reduced by an amount equal to double your Alchemy Supplies proficiency (minimum 1 day);
  • When making an Alchemy Supplies check, if any of the dice you roll come up as a 1, you can reroll one of them once.

You have a penchant for drama and the theater, granting you the following benefits:
  • You have advantage on Beguilement checks to pass yourself off as another person;
  • You can mimic the sound of another person’s voice, as well as accurately recreate a sound that you have heard using just your voice and mouth.

You’re quick on your feet, granting you the following benefits:
  • You gain 10m of movement;
  • Difficult terrain no longer hampers your movement.

You’ve become exceptionally competent at navigating the wilderness and finding your way where others would be lost, granting you the following benefits:
  • You gain 1 proficiency in Navigation, to a maximum of 5;
  • You gain 1 proficiency in Navigation Tools, to a maximum of 5;
  • You always know which way is North, granting you advantage on Navigation checks.

You’re extremely observant, keeping an eye on all the goings-on in your immediate environment, granting you the following benefits:
  • Your Perception proficiency increases by 1, up to 5;
  • If you can see a creature’s lips moving while it speaks a language that you understand, you can understand what it is saying.

You have felt the touch of one of the Others, and in so doing gained a modicum of eldritch power. You learn one Otherworldly Spell of your choice that has a Mana Cost of 0.


You are a master with ranged weapons, raining death and striking fear into the hearts of your foes from afar, granting you the following benefits:
  • You gain 1 proficiency in the Archery, Firearm, or Throwing skill, and that skill can now be increased to 6 even if another feature says otherwise;
  • Long and melee range attacks with ranged weapons are no longer made with disadvantage for you;
  • Your ranged weapon attacks ignore partial cover.

Pick a single Action or Tool Skill that you have 5 proficiency in; your proficiency in that Skill increases to 6. This feat can be taken multiple times.


You gain 3 SP to distribute between any Action or Tool Skill(s) of your choice. This feat can be taken multiple times.


Using this feat, you can learn a single class feature from any one class (except Aetherian) prior to that class’s Class Feat (Novice) feature. For example, you could learn the Warrior’s “Fighting Style” or “Second Wind” features, but not anything that they gain at any levels thereafter. You can take this feat multiple times but cannot take a feature more than once.


You are incredibly difficult to kill, causing your Health to immediately increase by an amount equal to your level. With every level you gain from now on, your Health increases by an additional 1.


Prerequisite: Dual-Wielding

You have trained and practiced with large, two-handed weapons, allowing you to carry and swing them with ease. So much ease, in fact, that you are able to wield two of them at once, one in each hand. This feat allows you to dual-wield weapons with the Two-Handed property, or wield them with one hand, leaving your other hand free. In order to wield a two-handed weapon in one hand, your STR must be >1 more than the STR requirement of the weapon; additionally, when wielding a two-handed weapon in one hand, the magnitude of the base damage die is reduced by 1, e.g. a d8 would become a d6.


Your body is toned, tough, and resilient to anything that might harm it. From now on, do not half the sum of your STR and CON scores when determining your Fortitude score; if you already gain this benefit from another source, your Fortitude save instead increases by another 2.


Your reflexes are razor-sharp, and danger never gets close to you. From now on, do not half the sum of your AGI and DEX when determining your Reflex save; if you already gain this benefit from another source, your Reflex save instead increases by another 2.


Select a single Weapon Skill that you have >5 proficiency in and that is not a class feature; you have learnt to wield those weapons as though they are extensions of your own body, granting you the following benefits:
  • Your proficiency in that skill increases to 6 if it wasn't already;
  • Whenever you make an attack with that weapon, if you roll a 1 on any of the damage dice, you can reroll one of them once and must use the new result.
If you choose Firearm, Small, Throwing, or Unarmed, you gain the following as well:
  • Firearm: You can now reload Firearm weapons in half the time, rounded down if necessary;
  • Small: You gain an additional action on each of your turns that can only be used to attack with Small weapons;
  • Throwing: You gain an additional action on each of your turns that can only be used to attack with Throwing weapons that deal < 1d4 base damage;
  • Unarmed: Your unarmed strikes deal 1d4 + your STR score physical base damage unless otherwise stated by another feature.
You can take this feat multiple times.


You gain 2 WP to distribute between any Weapon Skill(s) of your choice that are not class features. You can take this feat multiple times.
