Aetherian

Becoming an Aetherian

How exactly Sentients become Aetherians varies from world to world, and at the end of the day, it is up to your GM to decide. In the world of Aethra for example, the primary method of becoming an Aetherian is to ingest or inject Aether, and after suffering through the ensuing Aether Sickness, there is a chance the sufferer will come out of it an Aetherian. Other methods include being born on the appropriate plane, finding a relic that grants you Aetherian powers, being blessed with powers by one of the gods, or simply being born one. No one method of becoming an Aetherian is incorrect, unless it runs directly contradictory to the rules of the world that your GM has established; if your GM says that in their world, Aetherians are born with their powers, then you cannot make an Aetherian that learns their spells through tireless study (unless they allow it).

Control Over the Elements

The Aetherian is a special class: it does not have any class feats, but instead there are 12 different sub-types of the overarching class, referred to here as the Elemental Domains. It is also one of two classes with access to spells, and because of this has access to some of the most unique, powerful, and useful abilities in the game. An Aetherian can only ever have control over one Elemental Domain, unless they gain access to another through an external source such as an enchant.

The Power of Aether

Like the substance Aetherians' get their abilities from, the term Aether also refers to the spells they cast, the mystical abilities that they wield, the powers at their command, and more. Each Aetherian’s Aether is tied to a specific Elemental Domain, which they gain control over at the time of their transformation into an Aetherian, described in the “Becoming an Aetherian” section above. Each Elemental Domain is mostly restricted to its own abilities and powers due to their inherent differences, however there are some common spells, called such and detailed inside the class description.

Class Features

As an Aetherian you gain the following class features.

Health

Health at 1st Level: 6 + your CON score

Health Beyond 1st Level: 1d6 (or 3.5) + your CON score per Aetherian level after 1st

Proficiencies

Skills at 1st Level: You gain 1 proficiency in Arcana, which can now be increased to 6 even if another feature says otherwise.

Skills Beyond 1st Level: You gain 1 SP every time you level up and 1 WP every 4 levels starting at level 4.


Aether

At 1st level, you command one of the 12 elements, using that power to cast spells and create arcane effects at your will. These abilities are collectively referred to as Aether, named after the mystical substance from which you have gained your powers.

The damage dealt by your spells is determined by your proficiency with Aether, which for the purposes of damage can be considered an Exotic weapon that you start with 3 proficiency in; you will gain more proficiency in Aether as you gain levels in this class, as shown in the Aetherian class table. Aether's base damage die is a d8, so when a spell or effect says it uses your Aether proficiency for damage, you will roll as many d8s as your Aether proficiency (i.e. 3d8 at levels 1-4).

Lastly, casting spells and using Aether usually requires gesticulations and fine movements, meaning that you cannot cast spells or use class features while wearing any armor that grants disadvantage on DEX checks or shields.

Aether Save DR. Several spells and other features cause the target(s) to make a save against one or more conditions of the effect, whether that be getting pushed, keeping their thoughts shielded, retaining control of their body, or something else entirely. The DR for these saves is referred to as your Aether Save DR, and is determined as follows:

Aether Save DR: 10 + double your Aether proficiency + your Aetherian level

Elemental Domain

At 1st level, you have discovered your inherent control over one of the 12 elements: Air, Earth, Fire, Water, Death, Gravity, Life, Magic, Dark, Light, Space, or Time. These Domains have different abilities, spells, and roles both in and out of combat; additionally, each Domain gets additional features at 6th, 13th, and possibly 18th levels. Whichever Domain your character controls is up to you, but you should also consider how this effects their personality, their background, how they came across their powers, their struggle to control the Aether they now command. Perhaps your Ignimancer is hot-headed and quick to anger, or your Magician is coy and mischievous. Whatever the case may be, be sure to put some thought into how discovering, gaining, struggling with, and controlling their Domain has influenced your Aetherian’s life, background, and personality.

Elemancy

As an inherent part of their domain, Aetherians are able to do an incalculable number of simple effects relating to their element without expending any Mana, such as creating puffs of air, minor sensory effects, or dousing Tiny fires. Elemancy uses d6s for damage, and you can use it to deal up to 3d6 damage before expending Mana.

Mana

Mana is a numeric representation of an Aetherian’s mental fortitude, their remaining strength and will to wield the Aether at their disposal. It determines how much you can use your Aether before needing to rest and what spells you are able to cast. If the Mana Cost of a spell is > your remaining Mana, you can still cast it, however your Mana will be reduced to 0 and your current Health will be reduced by 5 times the remaining difference.

Your primary method of regaining Mana is resting; during a quick rest you can roll an amount of d8s up to half your Aether proficiency (rounded down), and a night's or day's rest will completely restore your Mana. Alternatively, Ethers restore Mana similar to how Potions restore Health. You also regain 10 Mana whenever you ingest ½ a pound of Aether of your domain’s type.

Ability Score Improvement

When you reach 4th level, and again at 8th, 12th, 16th, and 19th level, you can increase one of your ability scores by 1, two of them by .5, or take a classless feat. You cannot increase an ability score beyond 5 via this feature.

Enhanced Element

At 11th level, you have mastered your element enough that you have learned to control a more advanced form of it. The enhanced elements are as follows:

Air. Aeromancers are able to mix in trace amounts of ice and fire into their abilities, and as such, are able to change the damage type of their spells. Mixing in shards of ice changes the damage type to physical/freeze and sparks of fire to burn.

Earth. Terramancers are able to manipulate metal and glass to their wills just as easily as they are able to control raw earth or stone.

Fire. Ignimancers find themselves able to control lightning as well as fire. This allows them to change their flame spells to lightning and vice versa, which in turn changes the damage they deal from burn to electric.

Water. Aquamancers gain the ability to tamper with their water, making it poisonous or acidic at their discretion.

Death. The element of Death is final, and cannot be improved or altered. However, advanced Necromancers have learned the ins and outs of Death enough that they begin to learn about its complement, Life. Select 4 Life spells that cost < 6 Mana; you can now cast those spells as though they were your own. These spells are permanent and cannot be changed.

Gravity. At its strongest, gravity can bend not just matter to its will, but Dark, Light, Space, and Time as well, bending and warping them in ways none of the other Ethereal elements can. Select 2 Dark spells, 2 Light spells, 2 Space spells, and 2 Time spells that each cost < 2 Mana; you can now cast those spells as though they were your own. These spells are permanent and cannot be changed.

Life. Animancers, being greatly proficient with the element of souls, begin to understand what it is that ends them, that converse element that is so feared, Death. Select 4 Death spells that cost < 6 Mana; you can now cast those spells as though they were your own. These spells are permanent and cannot be changed.

Magic. The mysticism of magic is, by its very nature strange, ever-changing, and incapable of being fully understood, at least by Sentient minds. How, then, is such an element enhanced? In a way, it isn’t; it is simply altered. Lie becomes truth, falsity becomes reality, and illusion, through the power, training, and will of the Magician, becomes conjuration – though almost always temporarily.

Dark. Umbramancers and Luxomancers both dabble in the mirror of their element, infusing their Aether with a bit of light to create twilight. This allows them to learn a few spells of the opposing element, as well as alter their own spells to some degree. Select 4 Light spells that cost < 6 Mana; you can now cast those spells as though they were your own. These spells are permanent and cannot be changed.

Light. Luxomancers, like their Umbramancer brethren, dip their toes in the reflection of their element, infusing their powers with a touch of dark to create twilight. This grants them knowledge of a few of the opposite element’s spells, as well as affect their own spells a little bit. Select 4 Dark spells that cost < 6 Mana; you can now cast those spells as though they were your own. These spells are permanent and cannot be changed.

Space. The element of Space is seemingly the most orderly one: everything that physically exists seems to make sense, seems to belong where it is, it geometrically fits into the world – unless a particularly strong Mattemancer wishes it not so. Powerful Mattemancers can force the geometry of the world to obey their own will, making the laws of physics into something more akin to guidelines than actual rules.

Time. Time is an unceasing river that flows ever forward, and Tempomancers are able to but only dip their hands into the water. Strong Tempomancers, however, can use their slight bit of sway and control to cease or even ever so slightly reverse the river’s flow for just a moment.

Elemental Tongue

At 14th level, you learn and are able to speak and write the language of your element: Air learns Angelic, Earth learns Terran, Fire learns Demonic, Water learns Aquan, Death learns Mortuan, Gravity learns Gravician, Life learns Animan, and Magic learns Faen.

Dark, Light, Space, and Time do not learn a specific language, but instead learn a complex and indescribable array of empathetic and telepathic emotions and thoughts that can be used to communicate with any and all of the Darkened, Displaced, Enlightened, and Timeless.

Favored Spell OR Elemental Domain Feature

At 18th level, you can either take the feature described immediately below, or gain the third Elemental Domain feature for your domain. You DO NOT gain both.

You choose to learn a single simple spell, permanently embedding it into your mind so that you can call on it more quickly and easily than you could before. Select a single spell that costs < 2 Mana and does not deal damage directly or via its Notable Effects; from this point on, you can cast that spell (without modifications) without expending Mana. Alternatively, you can permanently make Elemental Strike d8-based instead of d6-based.

One With the Element

When you reach 20th level, you are truly a master of your element, nearly a demigod with nigh-unmatched arcane power. From now on, whenever standing in an area heavily saturated with your element or on your domain’s plane, you regain 1d12 Health and 1 Mana per minute. An example for a suitable environment for each domain is given here:

  • Air: An area of intense natural wind, or being in freefall;
  • Earth: Making physical contact with soil that has not left the world’s surface;
  • Fire: Making physical contact with natural fire;
  • Water: Making physical contact with natural water;
  • Death: A graveyard, or within 2m of someone who has died within the past hour;
  • Gravity: Increasing your weight to thrice its norm (this also renders you stunned);
  • Life: An area where life thrives, or within 2m of a Sentient that has been born or resurrected within the last hour;
  • Magic: Standing within 2m of anything arcane that is not one of your spells or effects;
  • Dark: An area of complete natural darkness;
  • Light: An area of natural bright light;
  • Space: Making physical contact with at least 1 pound of any form of Aether;
  • Time: Standing within a flowing river.

Elemental Domains

Every Aetherian discovers that they have control over a single natural element: Air, Earth, Fire, Water, Death, Gravity, Life, Magic, Dark, Light, Space, or Time. Each element, with a couple of exceptions, has its own powers, abilities, and spells.

Air

Aeromancers are able to push and pull the air around them in order to manipulate it in accordance with their whims. More powerful Aeromancers are able to mix in trace amounts of fire and ice to amplify their abilities.

Aeromancy

Since Aeromancers command the winds, their Elemancy centers around the command of wind and air.

Enhanced Aeromancy. At 11th level, Aeromancers can infuse bits of fire and ice into their air currents, allowing them to perform the simplest acts of Igni/Aquamancy and deal burn and freeze damage.

Aeromancy Features

At levels 6, 13, and possibly 18, your powers in Aeromancy have grown to manifest themselves as additional abilities. At each of the given levels, select one of the following features; you gain that feature permanently, and cannot change it later.


Your command of the aeriel currents around allows you to better protect yourself, increasing your Dodge score by an amount equal to your Aether proficiency.


Falling - or more specifically, landing - no longer presents a serious threat to you, as you are able to slow your own descent so as to land safely. When falling, reduce the amount of damage you take by an amount equal to twice your Aetherian level.


The element you control now allows you to speed up your own bodily movements, increasing your movement by an amount equal to half your Aetherian level rounded up.

Earth

Terramancers can take control of raw earth, stone, wood, and plant life and shape it in whatever way they wish. They are able to conjure raw earth from the Plane of Earth at will, or they can choose to manipulate existing earth around them. More powerful Terramancers are able to manipulate metal and glass to their will as well as earth and stone.

Terramancy

Terramancy is the art of moving and bending earth, stone, wood, and plant life to your will.

Enhanced Terramancy. 11th level Terramancers find the essence of the earth present in metal and glass and can place those materials under their control as well.

Terramancy Features

At levels 6, 13, and possibly 18, your powers in Terramancy have grown to manifest themselves as additional abilities. At each of the given levels, select one of the following features; you gain that feature permanently, and cannot change it later.


As your adeptness with manipulating Earth increases, you find that your Domain has started to influence your very anatomy. Your skin, bones, and organs become hardier and stronger, and you find yourself shrugging off attacks easier than you used to, increasing your Armor score by an amount equal to your Aether proficiency.


While you are in direct physical contact with natural earth, stone, or wood that you have not conjured, created, or manipulated through a spell or other feature and has not been removed from the world’s surface, your STR score increases by 1, up to 6, or increases to an amount equal to your Aether proficiency, whichever is greater. After 11th level, this extends to metal and glass as well.


You are able to absorb the nutrients you need to survive straight from the earth itself. If you are in direct physical contact for an hour or longer with natural earth, stone, or wood that you have not conjured, created, or manipulated through a spell or other feature and has not been removed from the world’s surface, you do not need to eat or drink water for the remainder of that day. You do still need to rest, and you can still gain the benefits of eating or drinking normally.

Fire

Ignimancers are able to control fire and bend it to their will with ease. Ignimancers can superheat the air around them at will to create the fire they need for their various spells and features, or they can choose to manipulate an existing source of fire nearby. More powerful Ignimancers are able to bend electricity to their will just as well as fire.

Ignimancy

Ignimancy is all about super-heating the air and creating great and powerful bursts of intense flames.

Enhanced Ignimancy. Upon reaching 11th level, Ignimancers gain control over lightning, vastly improving their ignimantic repertoire.

Ignimancy Features

At levels 6, 13, and possibly 18, your powers in Ignimancy have grown to manifest themselves as additional abilities. At each of the given levels, select one of the following features; you gain that feature permanently, and cannot change it later.


You have learned how to manipulate the flame that you control to keep you shielded from incoming blows. From now on, your Armor is increased by an amount equal to your Aether proficiency.


Your attunement to fire grants you certain protections, making you resistant to both burn and freeze damage.


Like the Scorched you share your element with, you feel the scalding fires of unending rage burning in your soul. Using 2 actions, you can enter a state of fiery rage that lasts for the next minute. However, these boons come at a cost: firstly, if there are no creatures hostile to you that you are aware of, you attack the nearest creature that you are aware of, regardless of who they are; secondly, when you exit this state, you take 1d8 base fatigue damage, scaled to your Aether proficiency. Between night's or day's rests, you can enter this state a number of times equal to your Aether proficiency.

Water

Aquamancers push and pull their Aether to control the ebb and flow of the water in their spells. Aquamancers can draw moisture from the air around them to power their spells or draw water directly from a nearby source. Strong enough Aquamancers can tamper with their water, making it poisonous or acidic.

Aquamancy

Aquamancy is the art of controlling water and ice as extensions of one’s own body.

Enhanced Aquamancy. From 11th level on, Aquamancers learn to tamper with their water, making it poisonous or acidic.

Aquamancy Features

At levels 6, 13, and possibly 18, your powers in Aquamancy have grown to manifest themselves as additional abilities. At each of the given levels, select one of the following features; you gain that feature permanently, and cannot change it later.


Your mastery over water prevents you from being poisoned, diseased, or sickened from a liquid source. You can choose to become intoxicated via alcohol.


You are able to use the ice you bend to protect yourself, increasing your Armor by an amount equal to your Aether proficiency.


You have become considerably more comfortable in your element, increasing your Swim proficiency by 1, up to 6, or to an amount equal to your Aether proficiency, whichever is greater. Additionally, you can now breathe underwater, but not in dangerous vapors or gases.

Death

The element of the end, the great equalizer, the great unknown, that which comes for every Sentient, this is the element that Necromancers (colloquially called Morticians) command. Where others find fear in Death, Necromancers find power, power that they use to great and mortal effect. More powerful Necromancers are able to incorporate the power of Life into their Aether.

Necromancy

Necromancers call Death home, and bend it – and the strange and morbid powers of undeath – to their will.

Enhanced Necromancy. Starting at 11th level, Necromancers begin to learn the intricacies of Life and how close it truly is to Death, allowing them to perform simple feats of Animancy and learn some Animancer spells.

Necromancy Features

At levels 6, 13, and possibly 18, your powers in Necromancy have grown to manifest themselves as additional abilities. At each of the given levels, select one of the following features; you gain that feature permanently, and cannot change it later.


Your closeness with death clues you into when those near you get close to crossing into the Great Beyond; whenever a creature dies or starts Enduring within a distance equal to 10 times your Aetherian level in meters, you automatically know about it and where it is. You do not know how they died, but you do know if they were a Sentient or not.


You gain resistance to rot and poison damage, and you now have advantage on saves against poisons, diseases, and being sickened.


Twice between night's or day's rests, whenever an attack brings you down to 0 Health but not does kill you outright, you instead regain 1d8 Health. This benefit stacks with other features that give similar benefits, and it is your choice which is used each time.

Gravity

Gravimancers control the element of pushing, pulling, and holding – particularly, holding the universe together. Without Gravity, the world as Sentients know it would collapse in and out of itself, falling apart entirely. More powerful Gravimancers are able to bend a fraction of the Quintessential Elements to their will as well as typical gravity.

The element of Gravity is fairly simple, and is primarily concerned with the pushing and pulling of creatures and objects.

You can create it a number of times between night's or day's rests equal to your Aether proficiency. A creature can see through this illusion by using 2 actions to make a Perception check against your Aether Save DR; if they attempt to interact with it physically (such as by trying to touch it) they will immediately notice that it is illusory when this fails.
On your turn, you can mentally command the duplicate to perform up to 4 actions without using an action; should you command it to “perform” an action other than movement, the action will not actually be performed (you can, however, command the illusion to speak with your voice). You could, for instance, tell the illusion to move 5m and “attack” a creature, however the illusion will not do any damage, and the illusion may be revealed for what it is.
If you and your duplicate are in adjacent spaces or otherwise side-by-side, any attack against you has a 50% chance of being aimed at the illusion until or unless the attacker realizes the illusion’s true nature. Likewise, if you and your illusion are attacking the same creature, all of your attacks have advantage so long as the target is unaware of the illusory nature of your duplicate.


The deceitful nature of your element allows you to avoid incoming blows with greater ease, increasing your Dodge score by an amount equal to your Aether proficiency.


Your affinity with Magic and the ways of the Fae clue you in to the true nature of things. You automatically see through illusions (you are still aware of their presence), and your Beguilement, Insight, and Perception proficiencies increase by 1, all of which can now be increased to 6.

Dark

Umbramancers command and bend the element of Dark, the element of silence, secrets, chaos, and freedom. They can block out the light of the sun in the middle of the day or silence the din of battle to a dead quiet, and this has led to many fearing and mistrusting them, and not always without reason, though Dark is no more inherently evil than any other element. More powerful Umbramancers learn to infuse a bit of Light into their Aether to create twilight.

Umbramancy

That which dwells in the dark, as well as the darkness itself, are under the control of Umbramancers.

Enhanced Umbramancy. When they reach 11th level, Umbramancers learn to incorporate motes of Light into their Dark, creating twilight and allowing them to perform simple Luxomancy.

Umbramancy Features

At levels 6, 13, and possibly 18, your powers in Umbramancy have grown to manifest themselves as additional abilities. At each of the given levels, select one of the following features; you gain that feature permanently, and cannot change it later.


You can now use 2 actions to teleport to anywhere within 20m so long as both you and your destination are in complete darkness, or only 10m if you and/or your destination is in dim light and neither are in bright light; you do not have to be able to see your target but you must have line of sight on it.


The darkness that courses through your veins manifests itself around you when danger is present, making you harder to hit and increasing your Dodge score by an amount equal to your Aether proficiency.


It is the very nature of the dark to hide things, and as a being of darkness, you are no exception. Your Stealth proficiency increases by 1, up to 6, or to an amount equal to your Aether proficiency, whichever is greater. Additionally, you gain arcane darkvision if you did not already have it.

Light

Light is the element of knowledge, security, order, and hope. It guides Sentients in their times of need and gives them the hope to succeed where others may have failed. Many associate Light with goodness and honesty, and for this reason are typically quite trusting of Luxomancers, though Light is no more inherently good than any other element. More powerful Luxomancers are able to infuse a bit of Dark into their Aether to create twilight.

Luxomancy

Luxomancers use Light not just to illuminate, but can use its warmth to burn others, or gain knowledge they should have no natural way of knowing.

Enhanced Luxomancy. Dark and Light are two sides of the same coin, allowing Luxomancers to create twilight via simple acts of Umbramancy.

Space

The element of Space is that of matter and material, of that which physically exists and takes up, well, space. It is orderly, stable, dependable, and, as one of the Quintessential elements, quite powerful, with unique abilities. More powerful Mattemancers are able to break the laws of physics with their Aether, as well as create new demiplanes consisting of their own Space.

Mattemancy

As controllers over all of that which is physical and takes up, well, space, Mattemancers have access to some of the most useful and versatile spells in all of Iron & Aether; specifically, they share many spells with Terramancers and Aquamancers. However, unlike those domains, they cannot summon the required materials for their spells from their domain's plane, and must cast spells using whatever materials are immediately present in their environment.

Enhanced Mattemancy. Advanced Mattemancy deals not just with manipulating space, but undoing and creating it, allowing Mattemancers to remove creatures and objects from whatever plane they are currently on or create pocket dimensions big enough to suit their needs.

Mattemancy Features

At levels 6, 13, and possibly 18, your powers in Mattemancy have grown to manifest themselves as additional abilities. At each of the given levels, select one of the following features; you gain that feature permanently, and cannot change it later.


Your attunement to the physical makeup of the world allows you to distill matter into the building blocks of the universe: Aether. This can be done during a night's or day's rest, and the amount of time this takes varies depending on the type of Aether you are trying to extract: Corporeal Aether requires 1 hour, Ethereal takes 2, and Quintessential takes 3.


You focus on the material that makes up your own body, hardening it to ward off incoming harm, increasing your Armor score by an amount equal to your Aether proficiency.


Your attunement to Space allows you to bypass the greatest obstacle to your spellcasting: summoning the required materials for your spells from their respective planes. From now on, you can summon the materials for a given spell from the required plane if you do not have them on-hand; between night's and day's rests, you can do this for free a number of times equal to your Aether proficiency, and after you have expended all free uses, you can do it again by expending an amount of Mana equal to half the Mana cost of the spell you are trying to cast, rounded up.

Time

Time is the element of the unceasing, ever-flowing river that ensures everything is constantly moving forward and that a cause will always have its effect. Typically, Time only ever moves forward; however, stronger Tempomancers are able to stop it entirely, or even reverse it just for a moment.

Tempomancy

To control and alter the flow of time is the domain of Tempomancy; while this usually results in slowing events down or speeding creatures up, Tempomancers can also conjure symbols of time passing such as shifting sands, flowing waters, or the cogs of a clock to exert physical influence. Lastly, part of having the very element of Time fused to your soul means that it doesn't affect you like it does others: from 1st level onward, you age at a rate equal to 1/your Aetherian level.

Enhanced Tempomancy. Advanced Time control allows one to not just speed up or slow down the passage of time, but also rewind it just a bit.

Tempomancy Features

At levels 6, 13, and possibly 18, your powers in Tempomancy have grown to manifest themselves as additional abilities. At each of the given levels, select one of the following features; you gain that feature permanently, and cannot change it later.


Whenever you rewind time by any means, you can choose for the condition and/or position of your body to remain the same as it was before the rewind. Between night's and day's rests, you can do this a number of times equal to your Aether proficiency; you can give one of your uses of this feature to another creature that you can touch, and it lasts until their next rest.


Your affinity with time allows you ever so slight glimpses into the future, granting you a bonus to your saves equal to your Aether proficiency.


Your enhanced control over time allows you to avoid incoming blows more easily, increasing your Dodge score by an amount equal to your Aether proficiency.
