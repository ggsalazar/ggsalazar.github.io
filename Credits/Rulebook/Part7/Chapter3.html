Chapter 3: Concoctions and Enchants

Concoctions

Given here are all of the concoctions within I&A, along with the type of Aether required to brew it and the typical cost to buy it; however, it is up to your GM to decide how many vials of a given solution a seller has, if any, and whether or not it can be bought anywhere else. Unless a concoction’s description says otherwise, it must be drunk or injected in its entirety for its effects to be felt. A single person can be under the influence of up to 2 concoctions at once, including concoctions with instantaneous durations; if a 3rd one is imbibed, the drinker is sickened for the next minute, at the end of which they immediately spend 4 actions throwing up and take 1d6 true damage. If a concoction requires two or more types of Aether, it needs at least 1 pound of each type to be brewed. To identify a concoction and its effects requires a successful DR 16 Alchemy Supplies or Arcana check, though the GM may wish to modify this DR based on the strength and nature of the brew.

To determine the cost of a concoction, use the following steps as a guide (if an NPC is selling the concoction, use the prices given):

  1. Double the cost of the Aether used to brew it;
  2. Double the cost of the other resources, or double the number of days to gather resources;
  3. Round that up to the nearest multiple of 10.

This should present a fair price for the concoction, though there may be other factors that modify the price of the brew at the seller’s discussion.

The described effects assume the imbiber failed the save; if they succeeded, the poison has no effect, unless they deal poison damage, wherein like in all other instances, this damage is reduced by the result of the victim's Fortitude save.

Enchants

Enchants are powerful and usually very helpful items – unless the item in question is cursed, though this is rarely the case (for a list of example curses, consult “Relic Curses” here). An enchant’s description will say its effects, power level, the Aether used to make it, and how it changes as its power level increases, if it can.

All enchants must be attuned to in order for the user to gain their benefits and know their capabilities. To attune to an item, the user must spend a number of hours equal to the enchant’s power level squared focusing on and bonding with the item in question; the exception to this is artifacts, which take a full day's rest to become attuned to. A person can be attuned to up to 3 enchants at a time. Unattuning to an enchant takes only an hour, and only one person can be attuned to an enchant at a time.

Because the amount of possible enchanted items is practically infinite, it would be impossible and impractical to attempt to list them all; instead, a few items will be given at each power level for each category of enchant to give an idea of what each type of item at each power level is like.

Determining the cost of an enchant is far easier than deciding the cost of a concoction; because so much Aether is used to create them, simply doubling the cost of the amount of Aether used to enchant the item provides a decent base to start with, and then the cost can be modified by the power level of the item at the seller’s discretion. Refer to the table below as a guide on modifying an enchant’s cost by power level.

Enchant Cost by Power Level
Power Level Cost Modification
1st Level + 1d4 x 10 Aeons
2nd Level + 2d6 x 12 Aeons
3rd Level + 3d8 x 15 Aeons
4th Level + 4d10 x 20 Aeons
Artifact + 5d20 x 25 Aeons

Destroying an Enchant

Non-artifact enchants are not particularly more durable than regular items, unless the nature of their enchantment implies otherwise, such as with certain armors. Should an enchant be broken or damaged, it will continue to function as intended, unless the damage was catastrophic. For artifacts, although they are more difficult to destroy, it is not impossible; to destroy them, there are two options: the first is to simply assail the item with overwhelming force and power, such as tossing it in an active volcano, smashing it repeatedly, or assault it with a variety of powerful and destructive spells. The second is to first disenchant the item, then destroy it as one would normally; the process for this is covered in the previous chapter.

3rd Level


Aether. Earth, Life, and Water.

Description. A homunculus is an artificially constructed being, not unlike an Automaton, except that a homunculus is a flesh-and-blood creature constructed of clay and mud instead of metal and wires (though both do require Life Aether). Also like Automata, Homunculi in the form of a Sentient will eventually Awaken, becoming sentient, thinking and feeling for themselves, though this takes about 5d10 years; until then it essentially functions as a flesh golem with just enough intelligence and agency to carry out tasks given to it by its creator. The creature, whether Sentient-shaped or not, has the same stats as its natural counterparts. Homunculi in the shape of small creatures are often referred to as familiars, especially when under the ownership of an Aetherian. If a Homunculus’ owner dies, it imprints on the next Sentient it sees.
A Player-Character can in fact be a Homunculus, though this will not mechanically change anything, as only Homunculi in the form of a Sentient will Awaken.

Higher Levels. At 4th level, the time it takes for a Homunculus to awaken is reduced to 1d10 years. As an artifact, it becomes sentient immediately.

Using another 2 actions, the user can pull the hole off of the surface they placed it on, returning the surface to its normal state and sealing the aperture once more. Any objects or creatures inside the hole at this time are forcefully ejected, taking 1d10 physical damage in the process. If the hole is placed on a surface < 1m wide, the size of the hole matches the width of the surface it was placed on.

Higher Levels. At 4th level, the cloth is 20cm in diameter, creates a hole 2m in diameter and 2m deep, and deals 2d10 damage when it ejects creatures and objects. As an artifact, the cloth is 30cm in diameter, creates a hole 3m in diameter and depth, and deals 3d12 damage when it ejects creatures and objects.


Aether. Varies.

Description. Much like the various gate-creating spells, a portal allows one to travel from one place to another, including across planes. To construct a portal that sends one to another plane, Aether of that plane's type must be used and another portal must be constructed (or already exist) on the receiving end that also has the originating plane's Aether type; for example, to construct a portal from the Plane of Fire to the Plane of Magic, the portal on the Plane of Fire must be built with Magic Aether and the portal on the Plane of Magic must be built with Fire Aether (a portal does not need to be constructed solely of Aether, just have Aether incorporated into the building material). A single portal can be built with multiple types of Aether and link to multiple other portals. For portals that go to the Central Corporeal/Ethereal Plane, any type of Corporeal/Ethereal Aether can be used. Additionally, the same Aetherian must imbue their will into both portals, starting with the portal that matches their Domain. And finally, portals cannot be always-active, but can be activated for up to a minute using any trigger of the creator's desire, such as a speaking a specific command word/phrase near the portal, bringing a specific object close to it, activating a specific sequence of glyphs on it, etc.

Higher Levels. 4th-level portals can be always active, or turned on/off using the same methods as activating 3rd-level portals.

4th Level


Aether. Life, 4th level and stronger; Earth and Water, artifact.

Description. A phylactery can be any container that fits within a 30x30x30 centimeter space, and allows one to store a bit of their life essence – a piece of their soul – inside it, ensuring their continued existence in the event their current body dies. A phylactery will not supply a new body for the deceased, but simply stores their soul for an indefinite time until a new unoccupied body can be supplied (or the phylactery is destroyed), such as a corpse, Homunculus, or Automaton that has yet to Awaken. An Undead being, such as a Skeleton or Zombie, does not qualify. In the event a corpse is to be used, it must have died of a cause other than old age and have no missing vital organs, such as its heart, lungs, or brain; if both of these qualifications have been met, then the power of the phylactery will restore the corpse to 1 Health, healing any mortal wounds. The unoccupied body must be within 3m of the phylactery to be possessed.

Higher Levels. As an artifact, a phylactery can create a... serviceable new body for the user. This new body forms over the course of 1d12 days, and is clearly not a normal Sentient body. In short, it has the appearance of being a half-rotted corpse, and while it will have all the necessary appendages (two arms and legs, two eyes, etc.), it will also have several chunks of flesh missing, such as exposed bones and sinew, a missing nose, etc. The power of the phylactery keeps the body alive, but only for so long; over the course of 1d12 months, the new body decays beyond the point of usability. The user can choose between a body constructed by the phylactery or possessing an unoccupied body.

Destroying a phylactery. A phylactery does not follow the ordinary rules for being destroyed. Essentially, a phylactery is the only 4th level/artifact enchant that can be destroyed without needing to be disenchanted, and can be destroyed as though it were a 1st/2nd level object. This is because the power of the phylactery is so extremely funneled into retrieving and containing the attuned soul – and providing it with a new body – that it leaves relatively very little power to protect the container itself.

Full suits of enchanted armor can take the form of any kind of armor.

Description. These boots increase the user’s movement speed by 2m.

Higher Levels. At 2nd level these boots increase movement speed by 4m; 3rd level increases it by 6m; 4th level brings it up to 8m; as an artifact, it increases the user’s movement speed by 12m.


Aether. Magic, 1st level and stronger.

Description. This cowl gives the user infrared vision.

Aether. Earth, 1st level and stronger; Space or Time, 4th level and stronger.

Description. This shield grants a +1 bonus to the user’s Armor score.

Higher Levels. At 2nd level, the bonus increases to +2; at 3rd level, it becomes +3; at 4th level, it becomes +4; as an artifact, it becomes +6.

Aether. Earth.

Description. This full suit of armor grants the user resistance to any one damage type except fatigue, physical, or true.

Weapons

Most every enchanted weapon can actually take the form of any weapon regardless of the name, e.g. a Biting Blade does not need to be a blade but can be any weapon. This does not mean that the weapon changes shape on command, just that when the GM decides to place a weapon in their world, it can be any kind of weapon that they desire.


1st Level


Aether. Light.

Description. This weapon sheds bright light in a 10m radius and dim light for another 10m, (de)activating at the user’s discretion.

Higher Levels. At 2nd level, it sheds 15m of bright and dim light; at 3rd level it sheds 30m of bright and dim light; at 4th level, 50m of each; and as an artifact, it sheds 100m of bright and dim light, causes all creatures except the user within 10m of it to be blinded, and deals 1 extra damage die of burn damage.

2nd Level


Aether. Air, Earth, Space, or Time.

Description. This weapon increases the user’s proficiency in the same type as the weapon itself by 1, up to 6.

Higher Levels. At 3rd level, proficiency increases by 2, up to 6; at 4th level, it increases proficiency by 3, up to 6, and grants a +1 bonus to attacks; as an artifact, it increases the user’s proficiency by 4, up to 6, and grants a +2 bonus to attacks.


Aether. Space.

Description. This weapon has 4 charges and can expend 1 charge to teleport the user to any unoccupied space they can see within 5m using an action. It regains 1d4 charges at midnight.

Higher Levels. At 3rd level, the number of charges increases to 6, the range increases to 10m, and the number of charges regained becomes 1d4+1. At 4th level, the number of charges increases to 8, the range of the teleport increases to 20m, and the number of charges regained becomes 1d6+2. As an artifact, it has 12 charges, the range is 30m, and it regains 1d8+4 charges at midnight.


Aether. Space.

Description. This weapon, when thrown, instantly reappears in the user’s grasp so long as they remain within 20m of it.

Higher Levels. At 3rd level, it reappears as long as the user is within 50m. At 4th level, it reappears within 200m. As an artifact, it returns to the user so long as it is within 1km of them.


Aether. Fire, 2nd level and stronger; Light, 4th level and stronger.

Description. This weapon becomes awash in flame at the user’s command, shedding bright light in a 3m radius and dim light for an extra 3m, and dealing an extra 1d6 burn damage with each strike.

Higher Levels. At 3rd level, this weapon deals 2d6 burn damage on every strike and sheds 6m of bright and dim light. At 4th level, it deals 3d6 burn damage and sheds 10m of bright and dim light. As an artifact, it deals 4d8 extra burn damage and sheds 15m of bright and dim light.


Aether. Any.

Description. This enchant has 5 Mana at its disposal and can use that Mana to cast spells from the Aetherian spell list of the same type of Aether that was used to make it, with an Aether proficiency of 3. At the GM’s discretion, it may regain all of its expended Mana at midnight every day, or it may be a single-use item. The user knows which it is when they attune to it.

Higher Levels. At 3rd level, it has 10 Mana at its disposal and an Aether proficiency of 4; at 4th level, 17 Mana and 5 proficiency; as an artifact, it has 25 Mana stored within it and an Aether proficiency of 6.
